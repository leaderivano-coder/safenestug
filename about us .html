Who We Are

The Safe Nest Project is built on a secure, privacy-first reporting platform (JAVASCRIPT + MySQL) that aggregates anonymized data from child helplines worldwide. Our dashboard provides real-time insights into emerging risks, regional gaps, and advocacy needs — helping partners move from isolated response to coordinated prevention.

The Safe Nest Project connects over 150 child protection partners across 130+ countries. Together, we turn data into advocacy and isolation into action. Every year, our members respond to 13 million reports and support 3 million children — but until 2023, they worked alone. Now, we work as one.

Every child has a voice. We’re here to make sure that the world hears, listens and acts upon these voices.

Our mission

As a global network we share research/data, knowledge and experiences to enhance the quality of response to children and young people in need of protection, support and guidance and to advocate for their rights.

Our vision

A world where the voices of children and young people are heard and taken into account, and their rights are realised and protected.

OUR STRATEGIC VISION FOR CHILDREN

SAFENEST Multi-Annual Strategic Plan (MASP) sets a bold direction for the next five years. At the heart of our strategy is a powerful commitment: to strengthen child helplines worldwide so every child is heard, protected, and supported. Our strategy is driven by collaboration, equity, and a deep respect for local context. We believe that child helplines are essential to every country’s child protection system, and we’ll continue to be their strongest ally.

Our Story

We were inspired by Nantumwe Mastula who used to work with IDI in areas of kabowa and katwe, I noticed that there was an increased number of school dropouts, early marriages, sexual abuse most especially to girls, drug abuse mostly in boys at early ages among others, so I thought that if we start this project it could reduce on such cases.

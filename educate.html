Uganda’s National Child Participation Strategy: A Child Friendly Version

Your right to be valued, seen and heard

In conclusion, ending child abuse in Uganda is not the sole responsibility of the police or social workers—it is a community mandate. By strengthening local child protection committees, empowering families through savings groups and positive parenting, and utilizing tools like the Sauti 116 helpline, a community transforms from a silent witness into an active shield. The path to a violence-free generation begins when every neighbor, leader, and family member decides to speak up, support a struggling parent, and prioritize the safety of every child. The question is no longer if the community can act, but when it will choose to do so.
